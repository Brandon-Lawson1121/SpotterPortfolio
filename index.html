What Is Spotter?

Spotter is a fitness accountability platform that helps users find workout partners based on goals, schedule, experience level, and motivation needs.

Personalized Matching

Users connect with partners based on shared goals, workout styles, experience levels, and availability.

Social Accountability

The app encourages consistency by giving users real people to check in with, train with, and stay motivated beside.

Accessible UX

The interface uses readable typography, high contrast colors, large touch targets, and simplified navigation.

The Problem & Solution

Many fitness apps focus on tracking progress, but they do not fully address the social side of motivation, accountability, safety, and long-term user engagement.

Problem Statement

Students and gym users often struggle with consistency because they lack motivation, confidence, accountability, and social support.

  • Users feel unmotivated when working out alone.
  • Beginners may feel intimidated by gym environments.
  • Finding reliable workout partners can be difficult.
  • Many fitness apps feel cluttered or overwhelming.
  • Users may feel unsafe or uncomfortable interacting with strangers online.
  • Some users lose motivation because workouts become repetitive and isolating.

Solution

Spotter solves this by creating a fitness-focused social experience centered around accountability, accessibility, and trust. The platform uses a swipe-based matching system where users can find accountability partners with similar goals, schedules, and interests.

The Blueprint: Designing For Connection

Spotter was designed around one major idea: fitness motivation becomes stronger when users feel supported by real people.

Simple Interaction Flow

Users can quickly move from profile setup to matching, filtering, connecting, and messaging without unnecessary steps.

Human-Centered Matching

Supportive Experience

The design encourages positive reinforcement, accountability, and safer social interaction.

The Brains: HCI Principles In Action

Spotter uses Human-Computer Interaction principles to create an interface that is intuitive, accessible, engaging, and visually appealing.

Color Psychology & Visual Hierarchy

High-contrast neon accents guide attention, improve interaction visibility, and create an energetic fitness-tech mood.

Consistency & Recognition Patterns

Repeated layouts, typography, colors, and navigation patterns improve learnability and user confidence.

Accessibility & Inclusive Design

Readable text, large touch targets, high contrast, and simplified navigation support a wider range of users.

Cognitive Load Reduction

Simple screens and minimal clutter help users focus on connecting with partners instead of learning a complicated system.

Recognition Over Recall

Familiar swipe gestures, icons, and button patterns help users understand the app without memorizing instructions.

Fitts’s Law

Large buttons and swipe zones make mobile interactions faster, easier, and more comfortable.

Ethics, Privacy & Safety

Spotter also considers the ethical responsibilities that come with social matching, personal data, and user safety. Since the app connects real users, trust and transparency are important parts of the design.

Ethics, Privacy & User Trust

  • Consent: Users should clearly understand what personal data is being collected and why before creating a profile.
  • Transparency: Match recommendations should be understandable so users know why they are paired with specific people.
  • Privacy: Users should control profile visibility, messaging permissions, and personal information.
  • AI Disclosure: Future versions should disclose when AI-generated recommendations or automated moderation systems are involved.
  • Inclusivity: The design should support different fitness levels, comfort levels, and identity options.

Safety, Moderation & Future Improvements

  • Current focus: The prototype emphasizes clear matching, user control, and a fitness-centered experience.
  • Future report/block tools: Future versions should include report and block options to improve user safety.
  • Age limits: Safer onboarding could include age boundaries and clearer community expectations.
  • Moderation: Future versions could include moderation tools to reduce harassment or misuse.
  • Privacy controls: Users should be able to control what information is visible to others.

WCAG 2.2 AA Accessibility Audit

The Spotter prototype was evaluated using accessibility-focused Human-Computer Interaction principles inspired by WCAG 2.2 AA standards. The audit focused on readability, interaction visibility, usability, and inclusive design practices.

Passing Criteria

  • PASS: Strong text color contrast improves readability.
  • PASS: Touch targets are large enough for easier interaction.
  • PASS: Clear page structure and visual hierarchy improve navigation.
  • PASS: Visible focus indicators improve interaction visibility.
  • PASS: Error identification helps users recognize incorrect inputs.

Needs Review / Future Improvements

  • REVIEW: Alternative text support for images.
  • REVIEW: Improved keyboard-only navigation.
  • REVIEW: Additional screen reader labels.
  • REVIEW: Accessible alternatives for drag sliders.
  • REVIEW: Reduced motion support for animations and effects.

Accessibility Design Impact

The accessibility audit reinforced the importance of inclusive interaction design throughout the Spotter prototype. High contrast visuals, readable typography, simplified layouts, and larger touch targets helped improve usability for a broader range of users. The audit also identified future opportunities for improving accessibility support and long-term inclusivity.

The Lab: Testing & Iteration

After building the Spotter prototype, we collected feedback from 16 users to evaluate usability, visual design, safety concerns, and overall user satisfaction.

4.13/5average ease-of-use rating
4.06/5average overall rating
75%would recommend Spotter


What Users Liked

  • The swipe-based matching feature felt familiar and easy to understand.
  • Users liked the app theme, color scheme, and overall visual style.
  • Several users described the idea as original and useful for finding workout partners.
  • The messaging system was viewed as a helpful accountability feature.
  • Users said the app was generally easy to navigate.

What Needed Improvement

  • Users wanted a stronger profile page with profile pictures and bio editing.
  • Some users felt the app looked too similar to a dating app.
  • Users requested report and block options for safety.
  • Several responses suggested adding age limits and better privacy controls.
  • Some users wanted better visual feedback when connecting or passing.

Design Changes Based On Feedback

The feedback directly influenced later design decisions. The prototype was improved by adding stronger profile editing, clearer connect/pass feedback, safety and moderation controls, more transparent matching, and a stronger focus on fitness accountability instead of dating-style interaction.
